================================================================================
CARLA DEEP DRIVING - FUTURE ROADMAP & PROJECT EXPANSION
================================================================================

Deadline: ~2 weeks
Goal: Expand the paper from a "model comparison" to a "complete autonomous system"

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. MUST DO (Phase 2): Path-Aware Navigation (Estimated: 3-5 days)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Currently, the winning Baseline CNN just follows the lane perfectly, but it 
doesn't know where it's going at an intersection. 

PLAN: 
- Update Data Collection: Ask the CARLA route planner for the next command 
  (0 = Left, 1 = Straight, 2 = Right, 3 = Follow Lane) and save it to the CSV.
- Update Model: Modify `baseline_cnn` to become `PathAwareCNN`. It will take 
  the image, the speed, AND the one-hot encoded navigational command.
- Result: The car can now successfully drive point-to-point on a map without 
  getting lost at junctions. This is the core requirement for a "full" pipeline.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. MUST DO (Phase 3): Multi-Task Learning Head (Estimated: 2-3 days)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Right now, the model implicitly learns to brake for red lights, but we can 
force it to be smarter using a Deep Learning technique called "Auxiliary Loss".

PLAN: 
- Update the model to output 4 things: [throttle, steer, brake, TL_state].
- The `TL_state` is a classification output predicting if a light is Red/Green.
- Why for the paper?: This proves that forcing the CNN to explicitly detect 
  traffic lights improves overall driving safety and reduces red-light running. 
  It shows a deep understanding of multi-task network design.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. HIGHLY RECOMMENDED: Grad-CAM Visualization (Estimated: 1-2 days)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Deep learning models are "black boxes." Teachers/professors hate black boxes. 

PLAN: 
- Implement a script (`visualize.py`) that applies Grad-CAM to your trained 
  model while it drives.
- This will generate a heatmap over the CARLA camera feed showing exactly 
  *where* the AI is looking (e.g., glowing hot over lane lines, or glowing red 
  over a car braking in front).
- Why for the paper?: Screenshots/videos of this guarantee an A+. It is the 
  ultimate proof that your model isn't just memorizing pixels, but genuinely 
  understanding the driving scene.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. NICE TO HAVE (If time permits): Robustness & Edge Cases (Estimated: 1-2 days)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Testing the model in conditions it was never explicitly trained for.

PLAN: 
- Run standard evaluation in Town05, but change the weather to Nighttime Heavy 
  Rain (which wasn't in the training set).
- Measure how much the performance drops. 
- Why for the paper?: Discussing the limits, failures, and "out-of-distribution" 
  weaknesses of your model shows profound scientific maturity.

================================================================================
